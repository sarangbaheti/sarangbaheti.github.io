amdhal, moore, brooke and 90-90 - empirical laws of computers

Posted on August 24, 2011

Amdhal’s Law Amdahl‘s law says a program can run on faster than the time required to execute its sequential sections. The Law is cast in the form of an equation that accounts for the time spent in the parallel (or vectorizable) and sequential parts of the code. The validity of this concept is easy to grasp. If a program is 80% parallelizable and 20% must be executed sequentially, the maximum reductionin execution time is by a factor of five (if the parallelizable code were run in zero time).
