C++0x is an international standard

Posted on August 13, 2011

I am thrilled as C++0x, will be called C++11, is now an international standard, as confirmed by Herb Sutter

The final ISO ballot on C++0x closed on Wednesday, and we just received the results: Unanimous approval. The next revision of C++ that we’ve been calling “C++0x” is now an International Standard! Geneva will take several months to publish it, but we hope it will be published well within the year, and then we’ll be able to call it “C++11.” I want to extend my thanks again to Bjarne Stroustrup for sharing his work with the world and continuing to help move it forward, and to all of the participants whose hard work went into achieving this important milestone in the history of a great language. Thanks!

Huuuuuuurrrrrrrrraaaaaaaaaayyyyyyyyyyyy…
